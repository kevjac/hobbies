Peanut Blossoms

Servings: 48 cookies Total Time: 1.5 hours

Ingredients
1/4 cup granulated sugar
1/2 cup packed brown sugar
1/2 cup creamy peanut butter
1/2 cup of softened butter
1 eggs
1.5 cups all-purpose flour
3/4 tsp baking soda
1/2 tsp baking powder
Additional sugar for rolling
2 bags of Hershey's Kisses

Let's Bake!

  1. Preheat oven to 375 degrees F. In a large bowl, combine granulated sugar, brown sugar, peanut butter, butter softened, and egg with electric mixer.
  2. In a small bowl, combine dry ingredients (flour, baking soda, and baking powder).
  3. Add dry ingredients in increments to sugar/butter mixture
  4. Shape dough into 1-inch balls and roll in additional granulated sugar
  5. Place at least 2 inches apart on cookie sheet lined with parchment paper or silicone baking mat
  6. Bake 8 - 10 minutes or until the edges are light brown.
  7. Immediately press 1 Hershey's Kiss into the center of each cookie and then let cool on a wire rack.

Red Velvet Nutella Cookies

Servings: 24 cookies Total Time: 30 minutes

Ingredients
1 box Duncan Hines red velvet cake mix
2 eggs
Halfway between 1/3 and 1/2 cup of vegetable oil
1 cup chocolate chips
Nutella for filling

Let's Bake!

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F and prep baking sheets with parchment paper or a silicone baking mat.
  2. In a large bowl, mix the eggs, cake mix and oil. Once the batter is thoroughly combined (the light brown clumps of mix are combined), stir in the chocolate chips.
  3. Take large spoonful of dough (about an ice cream scoop worth), flatten out. Place 1/2 tablespoon of Nutella in middle of cookie dough (I normally place in a pastry bag and pipe the desired amount).
  4. Then pinch closed, form into ball and place on cookie sheet spaced 2 inches apart.
  5. Bake for approximately 10 minutes. Cookies are finished when you can slide them, without the bottoms sticking to the baking sheet. Best served while warm.

Chocolate Crinkles

Servings: 36 cookies Time: 5 hours (included chilling)

Ingredients
1/2 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
3/4 cup granulated sugar
1/4 cup vegetable oil
2 eggs at room temperature
2 eggs
1 tsp vanilla extract
1 cup all-purpose flour
1 tsp baking powder
1/4 tsp salt
1/4 cup confectioners' sugar

Let's Bake!

  1. In a medium bowl, mix together cocoa powder, granulated sugar, and vegetable oil. Beat in eggs one at a time, then stir in the vanilla.
  2. Combine the flour, baking powder, and salt in a small bowl; stir into the cocoa mixture.
  3. Cover dough, and chill for at least 4 hours.
  4. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F. Line cookie sheets with parchment paper.
  5. Roll dough into one inch balls and coat each ball in confectioners' sugar before placing onto prepared cookie sheets.
  6. Bake for 10 to 12 minutes. Let stand on the cookie sheet for a minute before transferring to wire racks to cool.

Chocolate Covered Cheesecake Bites

Servings: 30 Cheesecake Bites Total Time: 2 hours

Ingredients
10 graham cracker sheets
1/4 cup confectioners' sugar
1 tsp salt
1/2 cup melted butter
16 oz softened cream cheese
1 cup confectioner's sugar
1 tsp vanilla extract
3 cups melted chocolate chips

Let's Bake!

  1. In a plastic bag, crush the graham crackers until fine.
  2. In a large bowl, combine graham crackers, 1/4 cup confectioners' sugar, salt, and melted butter, stirring until the mixture is the texture of wet sand.
  3. Add graham cracker mixture into a 9x9 baking dish lined with parchment paper. Press the mixture down with your hands to create an even layer. Freeze for 10 minutes.
  4. In a separate bowl, combine cream cheese, 1 cup confectioner's sugar, and vanilla, stirring until smooth. Spread the cream cheese mixture evenly on top of the graham cracker crust. Freeze for one hour.
  5. Transfer the frozen cheesecake onto a cutting board, peeling away the parchment paper. Slice 1-centimeter-wide strips horizontally, then vertically, creating little cheesecake cubes.
  6. In a medium bowl, microwave the chocolate in 20-second intervals, stirring in between, until melted and smooth.
  7. Dip the cheesecake cubes in the chocolate, dripping off excess (*scoop cubes out of melted chocolate with fork and tap fork on the side of the bowl), and set them on a tray lined with parchment paper. Repeat with the remaining cubes. Freeze for 15 minutes, then serve!

The Best Fudgy Brownie Recipe

Servings: 18 large brownies Total Time: 3 hours (includes cooling)

Ingredients
3/4 cup unsalted butter
4 oz semi-sweet chocolate bar, coarsely chopped
1.5 cups of granulated sugar
3 large eggs at room temperature
2 eggs
2 tsp vanilla extract
1 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
1 cup all-purpose flour
1 tsp salt
16 oz of semi-sweet chocolate chips

Let's Bake!

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F. Line a 13x9 pan with parchment paper enough to lift the finished brownies from the pan.
  2. In a microwave safe bowl, combine butter and two ounces of chopped chocolate and melt in the microwave stirring in 30 second increments until smooth.
  3. Whisk together sugar, eggs and vanilla. Once well combined add in melted butter/chocolate mixture.
  4. In a small bowl, combine cocoa powder, flour, salt, and remaining 2 ounces of chopped chocolate and chocolate chips and then add into sugar/butter mixture.
  5. Once mixed, batter will be very thick and should be spread evenly into the pan.
  6. Bake 30 minutes and use a toothpick to check for doneness ( a few moist crumbs when inserted into the center of the pan). If brownies aren't done, continue to bake in 2 minute increments.
  7. Remove from the oven and place on a wire rack to cool before cutting. .

Snickerdoodles!

Servings: 12 cookies Total Time: 30 minutes

Ingredients
1/2 cup softened unsalted butter
2/3 cup granulated sugar
1/2 tsp vanilla extract
1 egg
1.5 cups of flour
1 tsp cream of tartar
1/2 tsp baking soda
1/8 tsp salt
1.5 tablespons sugar
1.5 tsps cinnamon

Let's Bake!

  1. Preheat oven to 375 degrees F. In a large bowl, combine melted butter, granulated sugar, vanilla extract, and egg.
  2. In a small bowl, combine dry ingredients (flour, cream of tartar, baking soda, and salt).
  3. Add dry ingredients in increments to sugar/butter mixture.
  4. Chill dough and cookie sheet for 10-15 minutes in the fridge while combining additional sugar and cinnamon in a small bowl.
  5. Remove chilled dough and roll 1 inch balls of dough in sugar/cinnamon mixture.
  6. Place on on parchment paper or silicon baking mat on chilled cookie sheet.
  7. Bake for approximately 10 minutes.

Science in Photography

The science behind my photos.

Crepuscular rays arise due to shadows cast by clouds or from hills and mountains when the sun is near the horizon. They are seen due to haze in the atmosphere from dust, water droplets, or air molecules.

By using a large aperture setting, only light focused at the appropriate depth of field falls correctly on the camera. The light from shallower or deeper in the field is scattered when it reaches the camera and appears as blurred in the image.

At night, when the moon is near the horizon, it can often take on the appearance of an orange moon due to light traveling through more atmosphere which absorbs or scatters more blue light or shorter wavelengths while preserving longer wavelengths!

Starburst patterns are typically created using a narrow aperture and a point source of light. Normally the sun is too bright to serve as a single point source and can overwhelm the photo. However, partially obscuring the sun can enhance the narrow aperture effects and obtain the starburst pattern.
